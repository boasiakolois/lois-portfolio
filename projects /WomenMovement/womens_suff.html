Women's Suffrage Movement

The Women’s Suffrage Movement was a groundbreaking fight for equality, led by fearless women who demanded the right to vote. Champions like Susan B. Anthony, Elizabeth Cady Stanton, and Sojourner Truth united to challenge societal norms and push for change. After decades of protests and perseverance, the movement achieved a historic victory in 1920 with the passage of the 19th Amendment.

Women’s Rights Pioneers Statue by Meredith Bergmann

This statue in Central Park honors women’s rights activists, depicting Sojourner Truth, Susan B. Anthony, and Elizabeth Cady Stanton together in an empowering composition of unity and action.

Suffragists Display Banners at Washington Mews, 1912

This historical photograph captures suffragists holding banners in New York’s Greenwich Village, demonstrating their resilience and dedication to women’s right to vote.

Suffrage Poster Depicting Inez Milholland Boissevain

This striking poster portrays suffragist Inez Milholland on a white horse, symbolizing her leadership in the 1913 suffrage parade in Washington, D.C.
